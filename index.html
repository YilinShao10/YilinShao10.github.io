Yilin Shao



I am a third-year graduate student in Design Science at Zhejiang University, currently working with Guanyun Lab where I collaborate with an amazing team to explore innovative design concepts. My research interests span Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), tangible interfaces, and sensory design. I am passionate about how emerging technologies can change the way we perceive and interact with the world, fostering more meaningful connections between humans and their surroundings.

My recent work focuses on how morphing food can engage children in both food creation and learning through interactive, fun experiences.

Feel free to reach out if you’d like to discuss research or brainstorm creative design ideas together! You can also check out my photography on Instagram.
